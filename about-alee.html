"I help women embrace technology, and learn the art of coding!"

As a qualified Facilitator and Trainer, my passion lies in teaching women how to be unafraid of technology, learn code, and enjoy it!

My journey into Web Development started in 2001, when I was at Uni a long time ago studying Multimedia, because I wanted to be a Games Developer. The first time I ever was introduced to coding was when I built my first website in Flash - yes, you read that right, this dying technology was what I first learnt to build my animated website with.

For The Love of Coding

I was exposed to learning C++ at Uni, which both confused and intrigued me due to its sophistication, but this language made me interested in coding. My interest in designing websites came when I started my first blog on LiveJournal, and was part of some community groups where there were very few girl coders. At this point, I taught myself HTML and that is when I fell in love with Web Design, and building and styling websites. I abandoned my Multimedia degree to focus on the Corporate life, but I never lost interest in Web Development.

Girls just wanna have fun

In the years to follow, I taught myself not only HTML, but CSS and PHP (as I also used to run forums that were run on PHP). From there, I built a plethora of websites using HTML, CSS, designing templates on Wordpress and Blogger, skins/themes for forums, and designed graphics, banners and logos that I shared on LiveJournal and DeviantArt. Most of these were just for fun. Seriously, for fun.

Fast forward 10 + years later, a corporate career under my belt, and qualifications in Business Advertising, Training & Assessment, and currently completing my qualification in Software Development, it was time to explore what a career in coding could do for me.

Code your own path

I created this space to help make it less daunting for women not only step into the world of coding, but I wanted women to enjoy learning! Bringing my various experiences to the fold, my passion is to facilitate knowledge and excitement for you as women to get your nerd on and code your own path into Web Developmemt.

If you want to work with me personally to help propel you into a successful journey into Tech, see my mentorship program to see if we're the right fit to work 1:1! Or join us here at The Coding Sessions, where you'll have access to courses, masterclasses, and resources that will teach you the basics to get started with embracing technology, and/or building a career in Web Development!
